Why we must stop from smoking?

By adopting these habits, you can reduce the risk of premature death and potentially add up to 10 years to your life expectancy. Additionally, you can lower the risk of developing cardiovascular diseases, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), cancer, and experiencing poor reproductive health outcomes.


Nicotine is a powerful substance that triggers the release of feel-good chemicals in your brain. With repeated use, your brain becomes reliant on nicotine to feel normal. Over time, nicotine changes your brain's functioning, making it feel like you need nicotine just to feel okay.

A few tips from me

  • Make the switch from smoking to healthy snacking, but be mindful not to overindulge to avoid weight gain; always pair with regular exercise.
  • Always restrain yourself and refuse when people offer you cigarettes.
  • Even if you're feeling stressed, don't attempt it again because you've already stopped.
  • Always track your daily progress when quitting a habit.
  • Don't consider "that" as a solution for relieving depression and stress, as it will only make it worse.
  • The last thing is, don't make "that thing" something that deserves to be used as a self-reward. Look for something more positive to replace it because if not, you will never get out of this vicious circle.
